Subjects for Electronics and Communication Engineering

Subject title Subject details
Digital Logic and Circuit This subject deals with theoretical knowledge and the practical application of digital electronics, logic gates, and circuit designs. It includes the following topics:
  • Number System and Codes
  • Boolean algebra and Boolean theorems
  • Logic gates and implementation of Boolean functions
  • Simplification techniques and Tabular method
  • Logic circuits, Sequential Circuits, Encoders, Decoders, Multiplexers, and De-multiplexers
  • Memories, PLA and PAL, ADC and DAC
Analog Electronics This subject deals with analogue circuits, signals, and devices, and their practical application. This subject focuses on:
  • Specifying the small-signal equivalent model of transistors
  • Analysing transistor amplifier circuits
  • Designing multistage transistor amplifier circuits
  • Designing and analysing integrated circuit biasing
  • Analysing the frequency response of linear amplifiers
Signal & System This subject focuses on studying the mathematical analysis tools required for the study of signals, signal processing, system description, and system response. The topics included are:
  • Mathematical Methods for the analysis and designing of systems
  • Representing signals using time-domain and frequency-domain methods
  • Application of Fourier transforms
  • Describing signal transformations using block diagrams, equations, convolution, and transfer functions
  • Application of signal filtering, measurement, and control of the physical devices
  • Formal project documentation
Electromagnetic Theory Electromagnetic Theory is the subject having to do with the study of electromagnetic fields. The topics included are:
  • Electrostatics
  • Magnetostatics and Maxwell's Equations of Time-Varying Fields
  • Characteristics of Electromagnetic Waves
  • Concept of Transmission Lines
  • Vector Algebra
Digital Signal Processing This subject deals with studying the fundamentals of digital signal processing and the principle of digital systems. The topics included are:
  • Introduction to Digital Signal Processing
  • Realisation of Digital Filters
  • Discrete and Fast Fourier Transforms
  • Multirate Digital Signal Processing
Analogue and Digital Communications This subject deals with the study of analogue and digital signals and their representation as waves, and is communication-based. The topics included are:
  • Introduction to Analogue and Digital Signals and their Representation
  • Introduction to Analog Communication
  • Introduction to Digital Communication
  • Source and Error Control Coding
  • Multi-user Radio Communication
  • Satellite Communication
Integrated Electronics This subject focuses on studying integrated circuits, their technology, and their working principle in devices. The topics included are:
  • Analog and Mixed signals
  • RF / Microwave / millimeter-wave Circuits and Systems
  • Components and Packaging
  • Wireline Circuits and Systems
  • Biosensors and Bioelectronics
  • Sensor Interface Circuits
  • Applied Electromagnetics
  • Circuit/Component Modelling, Simulation and Measurement
Control Theory There are two major divisions in this subject: classical and modern, which are directly involved in control engineering applications. The topics included are:
  • Power Circuits and Electromechanics
  • Applied Complex Variables
  • Discrete structures
  • Probability with Engineering Applications
  • Digital Signal Processing
  • Communication Systems
  • Control Systems
  • Digital Communications
  • Introduction to Optimization
Communications Systems This subject involves analysing and designing communication systems focusing on digital communications. The topics covered are:
  • Time and Frequency Domain Analysis
  • Power and energy spectral density of communication signals
  • Sampling and quantization
  • Line coding, pulse shaping, and pulse and carrier modulation techniques
  • Transmitter and receiver design concepts
Fiber Optics and Satellite This subject is based on optical fiber and satellite communication. It is one of the latest subjects to be introduced. The topics included are:
  • Introduction to Optic fiber
  • Fiber Materials, Optical Sources, and Photo Detectors
  • Fiber Fabrication
  • Optical Couplers and WDM Concepts
  • System Design and Optical fiber Applications
  • Satellite Subsystems and Transponder
  • Satellite Link Design
  • Earth Station Subsystems

Elective Subjects for Electronics and Communication Engineering

Subject Subject details
Digital Image Processing This subject focuses on developing a computer system that can process an image. The input is a digital image, and the system is to process that image using efficient algorithms and produce an image as an output. Digital Image Processing deals with manipulating digital images via a digital computer.
Optical Networks Optical Networks is focused on the study of using optical fibers to connect computers or any other devices that can generate or store data in electronic form. It involves the applications of optical signals and optical fibers.
Wireless Broadband Communications This subject revolves around studying wireless broadband systems, wireless communications, and their importance in the age of high-speed internet. It also involves the study of Wireless Network Areas like WLAN and WWAN.
Satellite Communications This subject provides the students with a broad treatment of the diverse satellite subsystems that make up a complete satellite communication system. It also involves the study of differences between radio and satellite communications.
Power Electronics Power Electronics is a subject that deals with the design, control, and conversion of power in its electric form. This is based on the switching of power semiconductor devices. Power Electronics can be considered a combination of power engineering, analogue electronics, semiconductor devices, and control systems.
Computer Vision The subject of computer vision focuses on studying how computers can gain high-level understanding from digital images or videos. It involves understanding and automating tasks that the human visual system can do.
Internet of Things This subject involves the study of fundamentals of the Internet of Things (or IoT), its access technologies, design methodology, different IoT hardware platforms, IoT data analytics and support services, and its industrial applications.
Advanced VLSI Design This course provides students with the ability to design, implement, and test the state of the integrated art circuits. It involves the implementation of special structures for complex digital systems, automation and verification of the design process, and design for testability.
Data Mining The Data Mining subject refers to knowledge mining from data (i.e., knowledge extraction), data or pattern analysis, data archaeology, and data dredging. It studies the processes for extracting useful information from a bulk of data or data warehouses.
Natural Language Processing Natural language processing is the subject that studies how to build machines that understand and respond to text or voice data and respond with a text or speech of their own in the same way that humans do.
